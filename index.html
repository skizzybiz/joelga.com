The Beginning

Once upon a time there were two silly people – one boy from Maryland by way of Pittsburgh and San Francisco, and one girl from Ukraine by way of London and Cupertino and all number of places.

By fate (or whoever controls these things), they met one day.

3

They were both a little unsure and a little insecure,
but amazingly, they were just perfect for each other,
and they began a family, in San Francisco by the sea.

The family grew
little by little,
in the way that
families do.

First came a little ball of fluff who loved pancakes and ham. The ways of cats are inscrutable, but to the silly people who loved her, it seemed that she was never afraid to speak her mind or sing the virtues of her shiny coat. Above all, though, she loved to curl up and share her warmth.

Next came an even more curious subject. With a crooked tail and more than his share of toes, he seemed at first implacable. Then the calm turned to fear – of what, they could never know – but eventually, they convinced him that there was nothing to be afraid of, except perhaps the occasional stray breeze. In time, he discovered an unwavering love for a tiny mouse on a wire, which, though a short-lived creature, has borne a proud bloodline.

The family grew weary of their neighborhood, with its never-ending hustle and bustle and its concrete alleys and misty days. They packed up all their odds and ends and trekked across the bay, into Oakland, to live in a quiet cottage. They swept out the cobwebs, painted the walls, planted some plants, and hung up their knick knacks. With the sun shining in every morning on their tree-lined street, they were satisfied that they had found a home where they could grow.
